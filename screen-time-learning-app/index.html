Screen time parents can trust

Most parents feel conflicted about screen time. The research is clear: what matters is not how many minutes your child spends on a screen, but the quality of that time. Active, not passive

Every game in Lunelo requires your child to do something. There is no watching, no swiping through a feed, no autoplay. Here is what active learning looks like inside the app:

  • Trace letters with their finger — building fine motor skills and letter recognition at the same time
  • Count colourful animals and tap the right number — connecting visual quantities to numerals
  • Spot the difference between two pictures — training observation, focus and visual memory
  • Drag letters into place to build words — turning letter knowledge into reading readiness
  • Complete patterns — building the logical thinking skills that underpin mathematics

Tips for parents

  1. Play together first. Sit with your child for the first session. Talk about what they see and hear. This multiplies the learning value.
  2. Use it as reward time, not filler. “You can play Lunelo after dinner” creates a healthier relationship with screen time than handing over a tablet to keep them quiet.
  3. Set a gentle timer. Even quality screen time benefits from breaks. 15–20 minutes at a stretch is a good starting point for 4–5 year olds.
  4. Connect to the real world. After playing a counting game, count things around the house. After tracing letters, find those letters on cereal boxes. The app becomes a springboard.
  5. Alternate with other activities. Lunelo is one tool in your toolkit — not a replacement for outdoor play, reading together, or creative play.
Expert perspective: The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends choosing “high-quality programming” and co-viewing with children. Lunelo is designed with exactly these recommendations in mind — educational content that parents can engage with alongside their child.
