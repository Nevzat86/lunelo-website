Learn Norwegian for Kids

Whether your family is moving to Norway, you have Norwegian roots, or your child is simply curious about new languages — Lunelo makes learning Norwegian fun, natural and completely safe. 54 games with native Norwegian audio, designed for children ages 4 to 8.

Why learn Norwegian early?

Children between 4 and 8 are in a golden window for language learning. Their brains are wired to absorb sounds, patterns and vocabulary in ways that become harder with age. Starting early means better pronunciation, stronger recall, and a lifelong connection to the language.

Norwegian is also a gateway to understanding Swedish and Danish — the three Scandinavian languages share a huge amount of vocabulary and grammar. Learning one opens the door to all three.

How Lunelo teaches Norwegian

Lunelo is not a flashcard app. Every game is built around active participation — your child traces Norwegian letters with their finger, matches sounds to symbols, counts animals while hearing Norwegian numbers, and builds simple words by dragging letters into place.

  • Native audio. Every sound, letter and word is recorded by a native Norwegian speaker. Your child hears authentic pronunciation from day one.
  • Visual learning. Colourful illustrations connect words to meaning. Your child sees the animal while hearing the word — no translation needed.
  • Self-paced. No timers, no leaderboards. Each child progresses at their own speed with encouraging feedback.
  • Immediate feedback. Sounds and animations confirm correct answers. Wrong answers get gentle encouragement to try again.

54 games across 7 worlds

Lunelo covers the full range of early learning skills, all delivered in Norwegian:

  • Letter World: Trace Norwegian letters, match sounds to characters, learn the Norwegian alphabet
  • Number World: Count objects, recognise patterns, compare quantities — all in Norwegian
  • Shape World: Identify geometric shapes, colours and spatial relationships
  • Word World: Read simple Norwegian words, match words to pictures, build sentences
  • Memory World: Spot differences, remember sequences, train concentration
